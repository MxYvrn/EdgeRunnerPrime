Step 4: EdgeRunner.

Traces boundary paths through the active-tile set left by EdgeFiller
(Step 3) and records each path as a Directional Freeman Chain Code of
Eight Directions (DFCCE). A walk begins from a seed popped from the
active-tile set and steps one tile at a time along any active
8-neighbour, appending the absolute Freeman direction on the first step
and the relative turn (abs_dir - prev_dir) % 8 on every step after.
Tiles are discarded from the active-tile set as they're walked, so the
same tile isn't re-entered.

Branching (shared prefix): at a tile with multiple active neighbours,
every neighbour spawns its own continuation that inherits the chain so
far, so a Y-junction yields several chains that share their common
prefix.

Termination, two cases, both recorded in global_chain so nothing is
dropped:
  - Closure -- the next step would land back on the walk's seed tile
    and the chain has reached at least `shortner` steps (default 10).
    The closing turn is appended and the closed chain is recorded.
  - Dead end -- no active 8-neighbour exists. The chain so far is
    recorded unchanged.

Two functions live in the file:
  - edge_filler_checker rebuilds the active-tile set from a filled 0/1
    grid, for callers that hold the grid but not the set.
  - edge_runner is the tracer.
